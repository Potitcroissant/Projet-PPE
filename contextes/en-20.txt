   This 'medical gaze' is a false economy which not only leads to distress
   but contributes to poor medical adherence, missed appointments,
   maladaptive coping strategies (e.g. alcohol abuse, smoking, risk
   taking), medical error and poor decision making, high staff turnover
   and absences. My new book, Beyond the Medical Gaze: Practicing
   Psychologically Informed Healthcare, provides an evidence-based
   approach that embeds contemporary understanding in psychological and
   emotional health into healthcare practice.
