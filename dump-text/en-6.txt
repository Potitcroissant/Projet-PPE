Related Words

   Gaze, stare, gape suggest looking fixedly at something. To gaze is to
   look steadily and intently at something, especially at that which
   excites admiration, curiosity, or interest: to gaze at scenery, at a
   scientific experiment. To stare is to gaze with eyes wide open, as from
   surprise, wonder, alarm, stupidity, or impertinence: to stare
   unbelievingly or rudely. Gape is a word with uncomplimentary
   connotations; it suggests open-mouthed, often ignorant or rustic
   wonderment or curiosity: to gape at a tall building or a circus parade.

Other Word Forms

     * gazeless adjective
     * gazer noun
     * gazingly adverb
     * outgaze verb (used with object)
     * ungazing adjective

Etymology

Origin of gaze

   First recorded in 1350–1400; Middle English gasen; compare Norwegian,
   Swedish (dialect) gasa “to look”
